Future Value

Future Value formula : FV=PV(1+i)n

The resources,valueables or financial assets whose economic value or monetary cost would potentially grow over a period of time.
Future value of a particular possession assists in imparting the probable usefulness of investment that a person would consider to make.

Future Value Usage

Most commonly it is used in determining loans, payments and used in property, building or housing in general.
An ideal example would be a savings account in a bank, where the value of money increases depending on the amount that is been deposited daily, monthly or quarterly and interest rate of the bank.

Factors involving Future Value

1. Present Value of an asset.
2. Rate of return (RoR) that bankers/shareholders reckon.
3. Amount of time the investment is to be made.

Estimation of Future Value

Given the formula : FV=PV(1+i)n
and an asset which is a wrist watch that cost $100 at the moment

Where,
PV = present value of the asset
i = interest rate or annual net profit &
n = amount of time period of an investment.

If,
PV = $100
i = 2 %
n = 5 (in years)

Then,
first we get the base and exponent
our base is interest rate divided by 100 + 1
that is 2 divide by 100 + 1
we get 1.02
our exponent is number of years (n) which is 5
now solve the base and exponent
we get 1.1040808032
finally,
multiply this value with the present value,
1.1040808032 * 100 and we get our answer.
$110.40808032.
FV = 100 * (1 + 2 / 100)5 = $110.40808032
The value of wrist watch after 5 years (n) is $110.

Time Value of Money

Time Value of Money is a major concept of finance and is superset of future value. A set of variables specifically PV, i, n and FV together as a whole contribute to fundamental structure of Time Value of Money. Credit cards/Debit cards, car loans, home loans, or mortages in general depend almost entirely on time value of money.
